For my first professional portfolio artifact, I selected my AI Lab project, PathWise AI Assistant. I chose this artifact because it demonstrates my ability to use generative AI, research tools, custom GPT development, and design thinking to create a practical AI-based solution for a specific audience. The assistant was created to help graduate students studying artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data analytics connect their coursework to career readiness, project planning, resume development, GitHub portfolio building, and interview preparation.

I customized this artifact for an audience that includes my instructor, future employers, recruiters, and other students preparing for AI/ML careers. I presented it as a professional AI/ML career-readiness chatbot rather than just a class assignment because the goal of a portfolio is to show value, skills, and growth. This artifact allows viewers to see my process, understand the purpose of the assistant, and interact with a working AI tool.

During the creation process, I learned that a strong portfolio artifact should include more than the final product. It should explain the objective, process, tools, value proposition, unique value, and relevance. I also learned how important it is to think about audience needs when presenting technical work. This project helped me see that AI tools can be used not only for generating content, but also for designing useful learning and career-support systems.

The feedback and revision process helped me improve the artifact by making the bot more ethical, practical, and audience-focused. For example, the assistant was instructed not to invent resume experience, exaggerate qualifications, or provide legal, medical, immigration, or financial advice. I also tested the bot with prompts related to study planning, resume bullets, and project recommendations, then reflected on what worked well and what could be improved.
